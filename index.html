The intersection

Accounting and software, in one skill set.

Accounting graduates rarely build software, and software engineers rarely know the books. Hiring data shows firms increasingly want both in one person, and few candidates have both. That overlap is the focus here.

The path was accounting first — to understand how money and businesses actually work — then engineering, to build the analytical and financial tools around it. The approach is practical: accounting judgment decides what's worth building, and modern AI and ML do the building.

Selected work

Things I built and shipped.

01 · Model risk Live on GitHub

Independent Model Validation — SR 11-7

I took a machine-learning trading model I built, and validated it against the bank supervisory standard for model risk (Federal Reserve / OCC SR 11-7) — two independent tools (deepchecks + giskard), a formal report, and a fully reproducible environment. The verdict was not approved for deployment, and that's the point: the real skill isn't finding an edge, it's being able to prove to a professional standard that one isn't real.
